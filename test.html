Case Study

YoungLA — 8-Email Welcome Flow

An 8-part welcome flow designed for a performance and lifestyle brand. The goal is to introduce new subscribers to the brand, build trust, and guide them toward their first purchase in a way that feels personal and intentional.

Figma Email Strategy Wireframing Lifecycle Marketing Copy Planning

Project Overview

I approached this project as if YoungLA asked me to design a complete welcome journey for new subscribers. Instead of sending one generic introduction email, the flow is broken into eight purposeful steps that gradually introduce the brand, highlight key product categories, and build trust over time.

I started by defining the audience and their mindset when they first sign up—often coming from social content or a specific campaign. From there, I mapped out the journey in a simple flow: welcome & story, proof and trust, product education, social proof, and a time-bound offer to nudge the first purchase.

Once the structure was clear, I created low-fidelity wireframes for each email to establish hierarchy, content blocks, and the call to action. After that, I designed the full emails in Figma, keeping the layouts consistent with YoungLA’s visual style—bold imagery, strong typography, and clean sections that are easy to scan on mobile.

The result is a welcome sequence that feels intentional instead of repetitive. Each email has a specific purpose, clear message, and defined goal, while still working together as one story about who the brand is and why it matters to the subscriber.

Email-by-Email Breakdown

Each email has a specific purpose, content focus, and primary goal. The sections below are expandable so the flow is easy to skim without feeling overwhelming.

Purpose

Make a strong first impression, set expectations, and confirm that the subscriber is in the right place.

Content Strategy

  • Short, personal welcome message that feels human, not corporate.
  • Quick explanation of what YoungLA is about (performance, lifestyle, community).
  • Preview of what subscribers will get: drops, early access, training content, etc.

Primary Goal

Build trust and encourage subscribers to stay engaged with future emails.

Purpose

Prove that the brand is trusted and loved by real customers and athletes.

Content Strategy

  • Highlight UGC, athletes, and community photos.
  • Include short quotes or review snippets with clear takeaways.
  • Link to social channels for people who want to follow before buying.

Primary Goal

Reduce hesitation by showing that others already wear and trust the brand.

Purpose

Help new subscribers understand what to buy first and where to start.

Content Strategy

  • Show 3–4 core categories (e.g., training, pumps, loungewear).
  • Pair each category with a simple “who it’s for / when to wear it” line.
  • Use CTAs that drive to curated collections, not just the homepage.

Primary Goal

Move subscribers from curiosity into browsing specific products.

Purpose

Remove friction by addressing sizing, returns, and shipping expectations.

Content Strategy

  • Visual sizing guidance and notes on how key pieces fit.
  • Short FAQ block: shipping times, returns, restocks.
  • Optional link to a more detailed help/FAQ page.

Primary Goal

Increase confidence that buying once will lead to a good experience.

Purpose

Show how the brand fits into everyday life and different training styles.

Content Strategy

  • Curated looks for “leg day,” “rest day,” “travel day,” etc.
  • Use imagery that feels aspirational but still achievable.
  • Feature links to full outfits or collections, not just single products.

Primary Goal

Help subscribers see themselves wearing the brand in their own routines.

Purpose

Humanize the brand and connect with people who follow athletes or creators.

Content Strategy

  • Short profile or Q&A style layout with a sponsored athlete or creator.
  • Highlight their favorite pieces and why they like them.
  • Soft CTA to shop their look or follow them on social.

Primary Goal

Strengthen emotional connection and reinforce brand identity.

Purpose

Give subscribers a clear reason to place their first order now, not “someday.”

Content Strategy

  • Simple, time-bound incentive (e.g., % off, free shipping, or bonus item).
  • Reminder of what makes the brand different and worth trying.
  • Clear, singular CTA that drives to a broad but curated collection.

Primary Goal

Turn an engaged subscriber into a first-time customer.

Purpose

Wrap up the welcome journey and guide subscribers into the “normal” content rhythm.

Content Strategy

  • Thank them for being here and recap what they’ve seen so far.
  • Offer a gentle reminder of the brand’s main value props.
  • Let them know what type of emails they’ll receive next (drops, launches, content).

Primary Goal

Transition the subscriber into ongoing campaigns without feeling abrupt.

Key Highlights

  • Designed an 8-email welcome flow tailored to a performance and lifestyle brand.
  • Started with audience research, wireframes, and a journey map before design.
  • Built all emails in Figma with consistent hierarchy, layouts, and mobile-first thinking.
  • Defined the purpose, content, and primary goal of each email in the sequence.
  • Structured the flow to move subscribers from awareness → trust → exploration → first purchase.
